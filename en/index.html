YasuLab & Co.

Distributed Company that Works Remotely, focusing on Ruby/Rails Web Development, Education, and OpenSource.

Main Works and Contribution

  • Web Services and Mobile Applications:
    • Snapwhim, Visual Bucket List.
    • ScreenX TV, Real-time Terminal Broadcasting Tools.
    • Whistle on Android, Android app developed for 2011 Tōhoku earthquake and tsunami.
  • Education, Workshops, and Materials:
    • Lexues Academy, project-based learning course in Okinawa, Japan.
    • Digi-Lab Okinawa: Teach kids in Okinawa to code with Scratch and Raspberry Pi
    • ODEN: Okinawa Digital Educators Network
    • Rails Tutorial in Japanese, one of most famous Rails books in Japan.
  • Open-source Communities:
    • Okinawa.rb: Ruby User Group in Okinawa.
    • OpenSource Cafe: Open-source Cafe in Tokyo.
    • CoderDojo Japan: Open-source Programming Clubs for Kids in Japan.

About us

President: Yohei Yasukawa

He obtained the degree of Master in Information and Computer Science from Waseda University, and studied at Monmouth College in Illinois state, USA. He founded YasuLab in 2012, a small distributed company that focues on Ruby/Rails web development and its education with opensource mind (see LinkedIn for details).

Not only such businesses, but also YasuLab contributes to opensource communities like Okinawa.rb (cf. Okinawa RubyKaigi 01), Weekend Fabrication (Project-Based Learning in Okinawa), and Rails Tutorial JP. For such enthusiastic contribution to IT and Education fields in Japan, YasuLab's works were accepted by The MITOH Program (and one of us is accredited as Super Creator) in 2012, seleted as one of TEDxRyukyu talks in 2013, and awarded Outstanding Performance Awards at Fukuoka Ruby Award in 2014.

Now, with welcoming co-workers to YasuLab, we have two offices in Tokyo and Okinawa, Japan, and we work remotely.
